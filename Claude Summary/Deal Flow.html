Aadyah Aerospace Spacetech
Full-stack spacecraft subsystem designer and manufacturer building the US–India space supply chain corridor
72
Stage: Series A Ask: $5M Source: Deal Flow folder Reviewed: 2026-02-23 Needs Research
Score Breakdown
Team: 20/25 Market: 15/20 Traction: 13/20 Product: 12/15 Biz Model: 6/10 Deal Terms: 6/10

Key Strengths

  1. Exceptional team pedigree — ISRO veterans, US Space Force heritage (Dr. Palowitch ex-NRO), and demonstrated expertise across 6 engineering disciplines in-house
  2. Genuine traction: $1.68M revenue, profitable for 3 years, 15 customers across 9 countries, 2 flight-proven systems with 100% first-attempt success rate
  3. Real competitive moat — 3x cost advantage from ISRO supplier network, 3–6 month delivery vs 12–18 months industry norm, full-stack design prevents integration failures competitors can't solve
  4. $5.16M order book to be delivered in 14 months — 3x current annual revenue in backlog, largely from existing customers with high switching costs
  5. Credible US market entry strategy — US entity established, TAA/ITAR experience documented, identified acquisition target (ABC Precision, $5.3M EV at 0.78x revenue) with immediate Tier-1 aerospace customer access

Key Concerns

  1. Aggressive growth hockey stick — revenue projected to jump 4.4x in a single year ($2.5M → $11M in FY26-27). This math works only if the ABC acquisition closes on schedule AND ramps rapidly. Any slip kills the model.
  2. Acquisition-heavy use of proceeds — $3.18M of the $5M raise (63%) goes directly to the ABC acquisition upfront. That leaves ~$1.8M for hiring, Europe office, and industrial facility. Capital is stretched thin across three workstreams.
  3. FOCI/ITAR regulatory complexity is significant underestimated risk — the proxy board structure for majority Indian-owned US defence contractor is novel and may face unforeseen regulatory friction with DoD/State Dept
  4. No commercial/business development leadership visible in the team — four technical co-founders, but no identifiable sales or BD head for US federal contracting. This is a critical gap for a company targeting government primes.
  5. Valuation not disclosed — cannot assess dilution, entry multiple, or fund fit. Raising $5M now with plans for an immediate ~$15M follow-on creates near-term dilution uncertainty.

Missing Information

  1. No valuation / pre-money cap stated for this round
  2. No historical revenue growth rates prior to FY25 (is $1.68M growing fast or has it plateaued?)
  3. No gross margin breakdown by segment — Stage 1 vs Stage 2 vs Stage 3 revenue mix, and actual (not modelled) margins
  4. No burn rate or runway — company claims profitability but no cash position or monthly P&L
  5. No customer concentration data — who is the largest customer and what % of revenue?
  6. No unit economics detail — actual CAC, average project value by stage, and LTV realisation track record
  7. No cohort retention / repeat business data (deck claims "repeat customers" but no quantification)
  8. Existing cap table not disclosed — who are the F&F investors and at what terms?
  9. ABC acquisition diligence — no audited financials for ABC; only FY24 EBITDA and FY25 revenue cited
  10. Use of proceeds breakdown for the remaining ~$1.8M post-acquisition

Questions for Founders

  1. Revenue grew to $1.68M in FY25 — what was FY23 and FY24 revenue, and what drove the trajectory? Is the $2.5M FY26 target already supported by signed contracts or is it forecast?
  2. The FY26-27 model shows $11M revenue. Roughly $6.79M of that would come from ABC — what is the organic Aadyah revenue assumption, and what specific new contracts or customers underpin it?
  3. FOCI compliance for a majority-Indian-owned US defence contractor is complex. Has a US attorney specialising in FOCI reviewed the proxy board structure? Have you had any preliminary engagement with DCSA?
  4. Of the $5.16M order book, what percentage is from the top 3 customers? What is the risk profile if one large customer delays or cancels?
  5. The deck states profitability for 3 years — what is the current monthly EBITDA run-rate, cash balance, and runway without this raise?
  6. What is the pre-money valuation for this round, and how does it compare to the $1.5–2B 2035 target? What milestones does the $5M buy you and what does the follow-on $15M fund?
  7. On the ABC acquisition — have you completed financial and legal due diligence? What are the key risks to the deal closing, and what happens to your FY27 model if it falls through?
Recommendation

Aadyah is a rare find: a technically credible, profitable, flight-proven space components company with a genuinely differentiated position (full-stack, ISRO-cost-base, US-India corridor) and real traction. The team quality and order book are compelling. However, the Series A is structurally unusual — 63% of proceeds fund an acquisition that hasn't closed, and the growth model hinges almost entirely on that acquisition executing without delays. Before committing, request: (1) audited financials for both Aadyah and ABC, (2) FY23–FY25 revenue and margin history, (3) valuation and full cap table, and (4) FOCI legal opinion. If those checks out, this could move to High Priority. Next step: Schedule a call with Shaju Stephen and request the financial package before making a decision.
